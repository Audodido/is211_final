bad Times poetry

The 'cut-up method' refers to an aleatory literary technique in which a written text is cut up and rearranged to create a new text. The concept can be traced to at least the Dadaists of the 1920s, but was popularized in the late 1950s and early 1960s by writer William S. Burroughs as part of his "cut-up poems".

'bad Times poetry' scrapes headlines from the New York Times homepage and reassembles them into lines of (pretty bad) poetry
